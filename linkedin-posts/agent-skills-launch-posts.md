# Agent Skills LinkedIn Launch Posts

Date: 2026-06-09

Purpose: reusable LinkedIn posts for the four public agent skills.

## Research Notes

Current LinkedIn launch/post advice converges on a simple structure:

- Put the strongest hook in the first two or three lines because mobile readers decide quickly.
- Keep paragraphs short and readable on mobile.
- Start with the reader's problem before naming the product.
- Explain the product simply: what it does, when to use it, and why it matters.
- End with a clear link or small question, not a vague engagement ask.

Sources checked:

- Post Assembly, "How to Structure a LinkedIn Post That Gets Read": https://postassembly.com/guides/linkedin-post-structure
- MagicPost, "The LinkedIn Product Launch Post": https://magicpost.in/blog/linkedin-product-launch-post

## 1. Identity Votes

```text
Turn ordinary self-talk into a clear read on who you are becoming.

I made a small agent skill called Identity Votes.

The idea came from identity-based habits: every repeated action is a vote for the kind of person you are becoming.

But most people do not want another tracker.
They talk through their day, their routines, their avoidance, their conflict, their goals.

The agent can listen for the pattern already inside that conversation.

Identity Votes keeps a lightweight vote ledger while you talk, then returns:

- the strongest identity signal
- the repeated behaviors behind it
- whether the trajectory is aligned or misaligned
- one high-ROI next vote

It is built for moments when reflection is messy, but the next action still needs to be clear.

Repo:
https://github.com/myfeng10/identity-votes
```

## 2. Next Mode

```text
Know what kind of work is safe after your attention has already been spent.

I made a small agent skill called Next Mode.

Working with AI can feel effortless while quietly spending real cognitive energy:

- prompting
- judging outputs
- switching context
- debugging
- deciding
- holding the whole project in your head

So the question is not always "what should I do next?"

Sometimes the real question is:

"What mode is safe now?"

Next Mode reads sleep, timeline, AI-assisted work, context switching, meetings, food, fatigue, and decision pressure.

Then it gives one recommendation:

push, switch, recover, or stop.

Not a productivity menu.
Just a clear next mode when attention is already scattered.

Repo:
https://github.com/myfeng10/next-mode
```

## 3. One-Shot Positioning

```text
Turn messy work into a line people can understand fast.

I made a small agent skill called One-Shot Positioning.

This is for the moment when the work is real, but the explanation is too broad, too tool-heavy, or too long.

Most people do not need more adjectives.

They need:

- a 10-second version
- a 30-second version
- three proofs
- the hard-part answer
- a taste claim
- what not to lead with
- one sentence to memorize

The skill pushes the explanation toward evidence:

What did you actually build?
What constraint did you touch?
What breaks after the demo?
What should the listener remember?

The rule is simple:

Do not lead with the category.
Lead with the hard part you touched, then prove it.

Repo:
https://github.com/myfeng10/one-shot-positioning
```

## 4. Smart People Prep

```text
Know what to say before talking to someone who thinks fast.

I made a small agent skill called Smart People Prep.

It is for conferences, coffee chats, interviews, mentor meetings, founder conversations, office hours, and recruiting calls.

The problem is not just preparation.

The problem is walking into a high-context conversation and over-explaining before you know what the other person cares about.

Smart People Prep turns messy context into:

- one sharp self-intro
- three proof points
- one context-extracting question
- a relevant bridge
- likely pushback
- a behavior rule
- a follow-up line

The core rule:

Get context first.
Then say one relevant thing.

It is built for people who have real work, but need clearer language before talking to someone who decides quickly.

Repo:
https://github.com/myfeng10/smart-people-prep
```
